HARD LIMIT (binding, non-negotiable): this dossier covers CULTURE, HISTORY, LAW,
ECONOMICS and AESTHETICS only — the "designer drugs" concept, seed-bank and strain

## Research questions (from the rainstorm)
1. Origins of the "DESIGNER DRUGS" CONCEPT: where the term came from (1980s),
   the legal cat-and-mouse of analog laws — as history, not chemistry.
2. Today's design CULTURE on the botanical side: seed banks, strain NAMING and
   branding, "fruitful phenotypes" as a marketing/cultural phenomenon (names,
   lineage myths, packaging) — never how to breed them.
3. Synthetic side via PATENTS: how a drug patent works (claims, priority,
   20-year term, Markush structures) and whether patents involve VISUAL design
   or are purely functional documents. Patent-drawing as an aesthetic genre.
